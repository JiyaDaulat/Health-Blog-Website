According to World Health Organization (WHO), Mental Health is a "State of Well-being in which the individual realizes his or her Abilities, can cope with the normal stresses of life, can work Productively and Fruitfully, and can contribute to his or her Community".




HISTORY OF MENTAL HEALTH


In the mid-19th century, William Sweetser was the first to coin the term Mental Hygiene, which can be seen as the precursor to contemporary approaches to work on promoting positive Mental Health. Isaac Ray, the fourth president of the American Psychiatric Association and one of its founders, further defined Mental Hygiene as "The Art Of Preserving The Mind Against All Incidents and Influences calculated to Deteriorate Its Qualities, Impair its Energies, or Derange Its Movements".

In American history, mentally ill patients were thought to be religiously punished. This response persisted through the 1700s, along with the inhumane confinement and stigmatization of such individuals. Dorothea Dix (1802–1887) was an important figure in the development of the "Mental Hygiene" movement. Dix was a school teacher who endeavored to help people with mental disorders and to expose the sub-standard conditions into which they were put.This became known as the "Mental Hygiene Movement"

Positive mental health and well-being is a combination of feeling good and functioning well.

1. EXPERIENCING POSITIVE EMOTIONS :


→Happiness, Joy, Pride, Satisfaction, and Love.

What does Negative mental health look like?


→Bad mental health can have wide-ranging effects on various aspects of a person’s life, including emotional, cognitive, behavioral, and physical health. Here are some of the key effects:


A.EMOTIONAL EFFECTS :

1. Persistent sadness or depression: Feeling down or hopeless for extended periods.
2. Anxiety and excessive worry:Constantly feeling anxious or fearful.
3. Mood swings: Experiencing extreme highs and lows in mood.

B.COGNITIVE EFFECTS :

1. Difficulty concentrating:Trouble focusing on tasks or making decisions.
2. Memory problems: Issues with remembering things or staying organized.
3. Negative thinking patterns: Persistent negative thoughts or pessimism.

C.BEHAVIROAL EFFECTS :

1. Social withdrawal: Avoiding friends, family, and social activities.
2. Changes in eating habits: Overeating or loss of appetite.
3. Substance abuse: Increased use of alcohol, drugs, or other substances.

D. PHYSICAL EFFECTS :

1. Fatigue and low energy:Feeling constantly tired or lacking energy.
2. Sleep disturbances: Insomnia or sleeping too much.
3. Physical pain: Unexplained aches, pains, or digestive issues.

E. IMPACT ON DAILY LIFE :

1. Poor job performance: Difficulty maintaining productivity at work.
2. Relationship problems: Strained relationships with family and friends.
3. Decreased quality of life: Overall reduction in enjoyment and satisfaction in life.

How Can You Cultivate Your Mental Health?


→Your mental health is shaped by social, economic, genetic and environmental conditions. To improve mental health within society at large, we need to address the social determinants of poor mental health, including poverty, economic insecurity, unemployment, low education, social disadvantage, homelessness and social isolation.
→

On an individual level, there are steps you can take to optimise your mental health. The first step is identifying your existing support networks and the coping strategies that you’ve used in the past.
There are also small things you can do to improve your mental health and help you to cope in tough times, such as:


1. helping others.


2. finding a type of exercise or physical activity you enjoy (like yoga).


3. getting good sleep.


4. eating healthy food.


5. connecting with others, building and maintaining positive relationships.


6.learning strategies to manage stress.


7.having realistic expectations (no one is happy and positive all the time).


8. learning ways to relax (such as meditation).


9. counteracting negative or overcritical thinking.


10. doing things you enjoy and that give you a sense of accomplishment.
